About

Cristina da Luz is a Cape Verdean filmmaker and screenwriter whose work explores the intersections of African folklore, collective memory, and the supernatural. A graduate of Emerson College (BA in Media Arts Production; minor in Literature; concentration in Screenwriting & Producing), Cristina draws on her Cape Verdean roots and diasporic experience to tell stories that question how history and trauma shape societies.

She founded 32MM Studios—an independent production company devoted to developing African horror, thriller, psychological thriller, and science fiction narratives inspired by folklore and underrepresented voices. Her work explores the blurred line between reality and the supernatural as a lens for ancestral history, cultural perception, and healing.

Through genre storytelling rooted in African cultural memory, Cristina is committed to elevating African cinema and amplifying voices that have historically been marginalized in mainstream narrative.
